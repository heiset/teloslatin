Praeteritus Singularis II (Movement, Following, Returning) = Iter facit sapientem — “Travel makes one wise” — proverb

Ī ad tabulam! Īstine? = Go to the board! Did you go?
Ī = I went

Scrībe in tabulā! Scrīpsistine? = Write on the board! Did you write?
Scrīpsī = I wrote

Revertere ad sellam tuam! Revertistine? = Return to your chair! Did you return?
Revertī = I returned

Herī Stephanus iit ad tabulam, in tabulā scrīpsit, ad sellam revertit = Yesterday Stephen went to the board, wrote, and returned

Sequere mē! Mēne secūtus es? = Follow me! Did you follow me?
Tē secūtus sum = I followed you

Stephanus surrēxit, puellam secūtus est, cucurrit, cōnsēdit = Stephen got up, followed the girl, ran, and sat down

Vertere! Esne versus? = Turn! Have you turned?
Versus sum = I turned

Herī Stephanus versus est in utramque partem = Yesterday Stephen turned in each direction
