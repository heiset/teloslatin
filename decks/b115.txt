Cōgitāre et Dubitāre (Thinking and Doubting) = Dubium sapientiae initium — “Doubt is the beginning of wisdom” — Descartes (adapted Latin)
Fortasse amīcus meus domī est = Perhaps my friend is at home
Quārē iānuam pulsat = Why is he knocking at the door
Quid putat = What does he think
Putat amīcum suum domī esse = He thinks his friend is at home
Puer magistrum putat hōrologium redditūrum esse = The boy thinks the teacher is going to return the watch
Certē magistrum putat hōrologium redditūrum esse = He certainly thinks the teacher will return the watch
