Dē Cibīs (About Food) = De gustibus nōn est disputandum — “There is no disputing about tastes” — classical proverb

This tastes bad = Hoc male sapit
This tastes good = Hoc sapit bene

Titus eats an apple, Titus eats food = Titus mālum edit, Titus cibum edit

Whatever you see here tastes good = Quodcumque hīc vīdēs bene sapit

Titus drinks water, Titus drinks a beverage = Titus aquam bibit, Titus potiōnem bibit

These are foods = Haec sunt alimenta

Not everything is food, not everything is drinks = Nōn omnia sunt cibī, nōn omnia potiōnēs

Some are vegetables, some are fruits = Nōnnūlla sunt holera, nōnnūlla pōma
