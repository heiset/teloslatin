Tempora Diēī et Nōctis (Times of Day) = Omnia tempus habent — “All things have their time” — Ecclesiastes

Diēs = Day
Nox = Night

Māne = Morning
Merīdiēs = Midday / noon
Post merīdiem = Afternoon
Vesper = Evening
Crepusculum = Twilight
Gallicinium = Dawn
Media nox = Midnight

Quandō sōl oritur? = When does the sun rise?
Prīmā lūce = At first light

Quandō sōl splendet? = When does the sun shine?
Māne = In the morning

Quandō sōl in mediō caelō est? = When is the sun in the middle of the sky?
Merīdiē = At noon

Quandō sōl occidit? = When does the sun set?
Vesperī = In the evening

Quandō sunt calōrēs? = When is it hot?
Post merīdiem = In the afternoon

Quandō surgis ē lectō? = When do you get up from bed?
Nunc, octāvā hōrā = Now, at the eighth hour

Quōtā hōrā vestīris? = At what hour do you get dressed?
