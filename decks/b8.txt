Audīre, loquī (Listen and speak) = "A man should listen!" —Plutarch

Listen (s/p) = Audi / Audīte
Do you not hear Do you all hear me = Nōnne audīs Audītisne mē
I hear teacher = Audiō magister

Speak in Latin (s/p) = Latīnē loquere / Loquiminī
Do you speak Latin = Loquerisne Latīnē
I speak Latin = Latīnē loquor
Speak in English French Greek = Loquere Anglicē Gallicē Graecē

Draw the sun = Dēlineā sōlem
What are you drawing = Quid dēlineās
I am drawing the sun = Sōlem dēlineō

Say “It is the sun” = Dīc / Dīcite “Sōl est”
What do you say = Quid dīcis
It is the sun — this I say = Sōl est hoc dīcō

Write the word “house” = Vocābulum “domum” scrībe
What are you writing = Quid scrībis
I am writing the word “house” = Vocābulum “domum” scrībō

Draw a line under the letters “u” and “m” = Dūc līneam sub litterās “u” et “m”
What are you doing = Quid facis
I am drawing a line = Dūcō līneam

Do you understand this word = Intellegisne hoc vocābulum
I understand — or not = Intellegō aut nōn

Come to the board = Vēnī ad tabulam
Look at the board = Spectā tabulam
What are you looking at = Quid spectās
I am looking at the word “house” = Verbum “domum” spectō

Read the board = Lege tabulam
What are you reading = Quid legis
I am reading the word “house” = Verbum “domum” legō

Quintus erase = Quinte dēlē
What are you erasing = Quid dēlēs
I am erasing the word “house” = Vocābulum “domum” dēleō
