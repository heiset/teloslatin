Ad hortum (to the garden... ablatives) = When death comes, I want him to find me planting my cabbages, but not worrying about him or my unfinished garden. —Michel de Montaigne

Stephen goes to the garden = Stephanus it ad hortum (accusātīvus)
Stephen enters into the garden = Stephanus intrat in hortum (accusātīvus)
Stephen is in the garden = Stephanus in hortō est (ablātīvus)

I go into Italy = In Italiam eō (accusātīvus)
The cat enters into the kitchen = Fēlēs in culīnam intrat (accusātīvus)

I travel in Italy = In Italiā iter faciō (ablātīvus)
The cat is in the kitchen = Fēlēs in culīnā est (ablātīvus)

Stephen goes out from the garden = Ex hortō exit Stephanus (ablātīvus)
Stephen comes from the garden = Ab hortō venit Stephanus (ablātīvus)
