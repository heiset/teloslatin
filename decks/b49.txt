Nōndum (Not Yet) = Dum spīrō, spērō — “While I breathe, I hope” — Cicero

Go to the board! Aren’t you going yet? = Īte ad tabulam! Nōndum ītis?
We are going = Īmus

Write on the board! Aren’t you writing yet? = Scrībite in tabulā! Nōndum scrībitis?
We are writing = Scrībimus

Erase! Aren’t you erasing yet? = Dēlēte! Nōndum dēlētis?
We are erasing = Dēlēmus

Stephen is standing on his feet; Stephen is not yet sitting = Stephānus pedibus stat, Stephānus nōndum sedet

Stephen is now sitting = Stephānus iam sedet

Climb onto the chairs. Are you climbing? = Ascendite in sellās. Ascenditisne?
We are climbing = Ascendimus

Climb down from the chairs. What are you doing? = Dēscendite dē sellīs. Quid facitis?
We are climbing down = Dēscendimus

Jump. What are you doing? Are you jumping? = Salīte. Quid facitis? Salītisne?
We are jumping = Salīmus

Go to the window. What are you doing? = Īte ad fenestram. Quid facitis?
What are we doing? We are going to the window = Quid facimus? Ad fenestram īmus

Open the windows. What are you doing? = Aperite fenestrās. Quid facitis?
What are the women doing? They are opening the windows = Quid faciunt mulierēs? Fenestrās aperiunt
What are we doing? We are opening the windows = Quid facimus? Fenestrās aperimus

Close the windows. What are you closing? = Claudite fenestrās. Quid clauditis?
They are closing the windows = Fenestrās claudunt
We are closing the windows = Fenestrās claudimus
