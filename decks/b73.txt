Praeteritus Singularis IV (Doors, Windows, Before / Now / Then) = Initium dimidium factī — “The beginning is half the deed” — Horace

Ī ad fenestram. Īstine? = Go to the window. Did you go?
Ad fenestram iī = I went to the window

Aperī fenestram. Aperuistine? = Open the window. Did you open it?
Fenestram aperuī = I opened the window

Claude fenestram. Clausistine? = Close the window. Did you close it?
Fenestram clausī = I closed the window

Fausta ad fenestram iit et eam aperuit et clausit = Fausta went to the window and opened and closed it

Ad ōstium iī = I went to the door

Aperī ōstium = Open the door
Ōstium aperuī = I opened the door

Exī = Go out
Exiī = I went out

Stephanus ad ōstium iit, aperuit, exiit = Stephen went to the door, opened it, and went out

Prīmum surgō = First I get up
Nunc ambulō, anteā autem surrēxī = Now I walk, earlier I got up
Nunc sedeō, anteā autem cōnstitī = Now I sit, earlier I stopped

Modo ōstium pulsāvī = Just now I knocked on the door
