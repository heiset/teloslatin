Poscō, Cōnstituō, Imperō, Efficiō = Imperium est mandātum — “Command is an order” — adapted
Magister telephōnum ā discipulō poscit = The teacher demands the phone from the student
Discipulus telephōnum aegrē dat = The student reluctantly gives the phone
Dā mihi librum quaesō magister = Please give me the book teacher
Magister dēlīberat utrum det eī librum necne = The teacher considers whether to give him the book or not
Magister cōnstituit librum eī dare = The teacher decides to give him the book
Domina cēnam servō imperat = The mistress orders the slave to prepare dinner
Servus dominae mandāta efficit = The slave carries out the mistress’s orders
