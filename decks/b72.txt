Praeteritus Singularis III (Hands, Eating, Drinking, Up/Down) = Manus manum lavat — “One hand washes the other” — Seneca

Cape mālum. Cēpistine? = Take the apple. Did you take it?
Cēpī = I took it

Ēde mālum. Ēdistine? = Eat the apple. Did you eat it?
Ēdī = I ate it

Cape pōculum. Cēpistine? = Take the cup. Did you take it?
Cēpī = I took it

Bibe. Bibistine? = Drink. Did you drink?
Bibī = I drank

Ascende! Iamne ascendistī? = Climb up! Have you climbed up yet?
Nōndum ascendī, adhūc ascendo = I have not yet climbed up; I am still climbing

Stephanus adhūc ascendit = Stephen is still climbing

Dēscende! Iamne dēscendistī? = Climb down! Have you climbed down yet?
Nōndum dēscendī, adhūc dēscendō = I have not yet climbed down; I am still climbing

Stephanus adhūc dēscendit = Stephen is still climbing down

Tetigī manum = I touched the hand
Plausī = I clapped
Aperuī librum = I opened the book
