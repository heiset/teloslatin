Dare, Capere, Pōnere (Giving & Taking) = Dō ut dēs — “I give so that you may give” — Roman legal phrase

Give me coins. What are you giving me? = Date mihi aera. Quid mihi datis?
We give coins to you = Aera tibi damus
They give coins to the teacher = Aera magistrō dant

Take the coin(s). What are you taking? = Capite aēs. Quid capitis?
We take coins = Aera capimus
They take the coin(s) = Aēs capiunt

Put the coins on the tables. What are you putting? = Pōnite aera in mēnsīs. Quid pōnitis?
We put coins = Aera pōnimus
They put coins on the tables = Aera in mēnsīs pōnunt

I see you = Tē videō
Yes, you see me = Ita, mē vidēs

I see you (pl.) = Vōs videō
Yes, you see us = Ita, nōs vidēs
