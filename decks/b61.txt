Quaerere Librōs (Seeking Books) = Quaerite et inveniētis — “Seek and you will find” — Biblical Latin

Seek your books. What are you seeking? = Quaerite librōs vestrōs. Quid quaeritis?
We seek our books = Librōs nostrōs quaerimus
The boys seek their books = Puerī librōs suōs quaerunt

Give me your books = Date mihi librōs vestrōs
The boys give their books to the teacher = Puerī librōs suōs magistrō dant

Take your books = Capite librōs vestrōs
The boys take their books = Puerī librōs suōs capiunt

Quot? et Numerī (Numbers & Possession) = Omnia numerō constant — “Everything depends on number” — Pythagoras

How many pens do you have? = Quot calamī sunt vōbīs?
We have three pens = Trēs calamī nōbīs sunt

Give us two pens = Date nōbīs duōs calamōs

How many books do you have? = Quot librī sunt vōbīs?
We have four books = Quattuor librī nōbīs sunt

Give us two books = Date nōbīs duōs librōs
