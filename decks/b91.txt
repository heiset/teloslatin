Dē Possessiōne Plūrālī (Plural Possession) = Res suās quisque amat — “Each person loves his own things” — Terence
Cuius est haec dormītōria = Whose pajamas are these
Haec est dormītōria mea = These are my pajamas
Dormītōria Quīntī est = They are Quintus’s pajamas
Quīntus dormītōria sua videt = Quintus sees his own pajamas
Quōrum sunt hī calceī = Whose shoes are these
Hī sunt calceī nostrī = These are our shoes
Heus puerī, capite calceōs vestrōs = Hey boys, take your shoes
Calceī puerōrum sunt = They are the boys’ shoes
Puerī calceōs suōs vident = The boys see their own shoes
Quōrum sunt hae camīsiae = Whose shirts are these
Hae sunt camīsiae nostrae = These are our shirts
Camīsiae puerōrum sunt = They are the boys’ shirts
Puerī camīsiās suās vident = The boys see their own shirts
