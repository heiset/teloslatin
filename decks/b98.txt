Prūdēns et Stultus (Wise and Foolish) = Stultitia est contrāriā sapientiae — “Foolishness is the opposite of wisdom” — Cicero
Prūdēns vidētur = He seems wise
At rē vērā nōn est prūdēns = But in reality he is not wise
Prūdēns vidētur at rē vērā stultus est = He seems wise but in reality he is foolish
Utrum bonus an malus esse volō = Do I want to be good or bad
Bonus esse volō = I want to be good
