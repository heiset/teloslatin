Dē Multiplicibus Locīs (In Urbe) = Omnēs viae Rōmam dūcunt — “All roads lead to Rome” — proverb

Ubi est cursus pūblicus? = Where is the bus stop?
Exī ex hortō et viam trānsī = Leave the garden and cross the street
Cursus pūblicus prō tē est = The bus stop is in front of you

Ubi est bibliothēca? = Where is the library?
Exī et ambulā usque ad compita = Leave and walk to the crossroads
Flecte ad dextram = Turn right
Bibliothēca ante pharmacopōlium est = The library is in front of the pharmacy

Ubi est pīstrīna? = Where is the bakery?
Flecte ad laevam = Turn left
Pīstrīna post theātrum est = The bakery is behind the theater
