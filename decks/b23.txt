ī/Venī (Come/Go) = “Fortune, I have got the better of thee, and have made all the avenues so sure thou canst not come at me.” —Cicero, Tusc. Quaes., v. 9.

Where do you come from? = Unde venīs?
Where does he come from? = Unde venit?
I come from the city = Veniō ab urbe
I come from the garden = Veniō ab hortō
Where do I come from? = Unde veniō?
You come from the school = Venīs ā scholā


Come here, Stephen! = Venī hūc, Stephane!
I’m coming = Veniō
Stephen comes here = Stephanus hūc venit

Go away from here, Stephen! = Abī hinc, Stephane!
I’m going away = Abeō hinc
Stephen goes away from here = Stephanus abit hinc

Titus, come in here! = Tite, intrā hūc!
All right, I enter here = Bene, intrō hūc
Titus enters here = Titus intrat hūc

Tertia, go out from here! = Tertia, exī hinc!
I go out = Hinc exeō
Tertia goes out from here = Tertia exit hinc

Stephen goes there = Stephanus it illūc
