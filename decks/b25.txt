Exī/Intrā (Exit/Enter) = “A reasonable exit.” —Diogenes Laertius, Life of Zeno

Go out of the room! = Exī ē conclāvī!
Where does the student go out from? = Unde exit discipulus?
He goes out from the room = Ē conclāvī exit

Enter the room! = Intrā in conclāve!
Where does the boy enter? = Quō intrat puer?
He enters the room = In conclāve intrat

Where are the bathrooms? = Ubī sunt latrīnae?
