Imperātīvus Plūrālis (Plural Imperative) = Imperāre sibi maximum imperium est — “To command oneself is the greatest command” — Seneca

Stand up! Are you standing? = Surgite! Surgitisne?
We are standing = Surgimus
Who stands up? The boys do = Quī surgunt? Puerī surgunt

Walk! Are you walking? = Ambulāte! Ambulātisne?
We walk = Ambulāmus
Who walks? The boys walk = Quī ambulant? Puerī ambulant

Sit down! Are you sitting? = Cōnsīditē! Cōnsīdistisne?
We sit = Cōnsīdimus
Who sits? The boys sit = Quī cōnsidunt? Puerī cōnsidunt

Sit here = Cōnsīditē hīc
Sit there = Cōnsīditē illīc
Sit on the table = Cōnsīditē in mēnsā
The boys sit on the table = Puerī in mēnsā cōnsidunt

Do not sit on the table! But sit in the chairs = Nōlīte cōnsīdere in mēnsā! In sellīs autem cōnsīditē

Do not jump here! Jump there = Nōlīte saltāre hīc! Saltitē illīc
