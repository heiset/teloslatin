Dē Prōpōsitiōnibus Complētīvīs (Object Clauses) = Scīre est meminisse — “To know is to remember” — Cicero

The teacher orders me to come = Magister iubet mē venīre
The teacher tells me to come = Magister mihi dīcit ut veniam
I do not know what to do = Nesciō quid agam
Ask why he is laughing = Quaerē cūr rīdeat
The teacher asks who is reading = Magister quaerit quis legat
I do not doubt that she loves him = Nōn dubitō quīn amet
