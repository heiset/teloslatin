Rogō quōmodo; Doceō; Discō = Docendō discimus — “By teaching we learn” — Seneca
Quōmodo mēnsam instruunt tata = How do they set the table dad
Prīmum catīnōs pōnunt deinde pōcula deinde cultrōs = First they put plates then cups then knives
Puer patrem rogat quōmodo hominēs mēnsam instruant = The boy asks his father how people set the table
Pater vērō eī expōnit quōmodo hominēs mēnsam instruant = The father explains to him how people set the table
Discipulī grammaticam discunt = The students learn grammar
Magister discipulōs grammaticam docet = The teacher teaches the students grammar
