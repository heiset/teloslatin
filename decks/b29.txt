Ad Ōstium (Going out) = “That I may go out better...” —Plato (when asked why he studies philosophy at the end of his life)

Go up. Are you going up? = Ascende. Ascendisne?
Yes, I’m going up = Ita, ascendō
Stephen goes up = Stephanus ascendit

Go down. Are you going down? = Dēscende. Dēscendisne?
Yes, I’m going down = Ita, dēscendō
Stephen goes down = Stephanus dēscendit

I’m coming. Come to the window! Are you coming? = Veniō. Venī ad fenestram! Venīsne?
Fausta comes to the window = Fausta ad fenestram venit

Open the window! Are you opening it? = Aperī fenestram! Aperīsne?
I’m opening it = Aperiō
Fausta opens the window = Fausta fenestram aperit

Close the window! Are you closing it? = Claude fenestram! Claudisne?
I’m closing it = Claudō
Fausta closes the window = Fausta fenestram claudit

I’m coming. Come to the door! Are you coming? = Veniō. Venī ad ōstium! Venīsne?
Stephen comes to the door = Stephanus ad ōstium venit

Open the door! Are you opening it? = Aperī ōstium! Aperīsne?
I’m opening it = Aperiō
Stephen opens the door = Stephanus ōstium aperit

Go out! Are you going out? = Exī. Exīsne?
I’m going out = Exeō
The boy goes out. The girl points to the door = Puer exit. Puella ōstium mōnstrat
