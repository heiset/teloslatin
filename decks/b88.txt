Dē Prōnōminibus Reflexīs (Reflexive Pronouns) = Nōsce tē ipsum — “Know thyself” — Delphic maxim
In speculō mē spectō = I look at myself in the mirror
Tēne ipsam in speculō spectās, Rosa = Do you look at yourself in the mirror, Rosa
Rosa in speculō sē spectat = Rosa looks at herself in the mirror
Pulcher sum, magnus sum, fortis sum = I am handsome, I am big, I am strong
Certē mē ipsum laudō = Certainly I praise myself
Num tē ipsum laudās = Do you praise yourself
Ille equus sē ipsum laudat = That horse praises itself
Ego Nicolāus sum = I am Nicholas
Vōbīs canere possum = I can sing for you all
Quid Nicolāus facit = What is Nicholas doing
Nicolāus sē ipsum commendat = Nicholas recommends himself
