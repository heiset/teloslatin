Dē Possessiōne Singularī (Singular Possession) = Sua cuique — “To each his own” — Cicero
Cuius est haec tunica = Whose tunic is this
Haec est tunica mea = This is my tunic
Tunica Quīntī est = It is Quintus’s tunic
Quīntus tunicam suam videt = Quintus sees his own tunic
Cuius est haec castula = Whose dress is this
Haec est castula mea = This is my dress
Heus Decima, cape castulam tuam = Hey Decima, take your dress
Castula Decimae est = It is Decima’s dress
Decima castulam suam videt = Decima sees her own dress
Cuius est hic amictus = Whose coat is this
Hic est amictus meus = This is my coat
Amictus Quīntī est = It is Quintus’s coat
Quīntus amictum suum videt = Quintus sees his own coat
