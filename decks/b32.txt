Review Dialogue Medley (Eō/Ī/It) = “When I review, I see one passage after another that should be erased.” —Ovid

Look for your book! Are you looking for it? = Quaere librum tuum! Quaerisne?
I’m looking for it = Quaerō
Stephen looks for his book = Stephanus librum suum quaerit

Now I find the book = Nunc librum inveniō
Stephen finds the book = Stephanus librum invenit

Stephen, give me your book! Are you giving it to me? = Stephane, dā mihi librum tuum! Dāsne mihi?
I’m giving it to you = Dō tibi
Stephen gives the book to the teacher = Stephanus dat librum magistrō

Stephen, receive your book! Are you receiving it? = Stephane, accipe librum tuum! Accipisne?
I’m receiving it = Accipiō
Stephen receives his book = Stephanus accipit librum suum

Open your mouth! Are you opening it? = Aperī ōs! Aperīsne?
I’m opening it = Aperiō
Paul opens his mouth = Paulus ōs aperit

Speak! Are you speaking? = Loquere! Loquerisne?
I’m speaking = Loquor
Paul speaks = Paulus loquitur

Shout! Are you shouting? = Clāmā! Clāmāsne?
I’m shouting = Clāmō
Paul shouts = Paulus clāmat

Sing! Are you singing? = Cane! Canisne?
I’m singing = Canō
Paul sings = Paulus canit

Whistle! Are you whistling? = Sībilā! Sībilāsne?
I’m whistling = Sībilō
Paul whistles = Paulus sībilat

Bark! Are you barking? = Lātrā! Lātrāsne?
I’m barking = Lātrō
Paul barks = Paulus lātrat
