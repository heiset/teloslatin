Motus et Actiōnēs (Movement and Actions) = Actiōnēs hominem ostendunt — “Actions reveal the man” — proverb
Vertere ad laevam = Turn to the left
Stephanus ad laevam vertitur = Stephanus turns to the left
Ambulā = Walk
Ad laevam versus Stephanus ambulat = Turned to the left Stephanus walks
Revertere ad sellam tuam = Return to your chair
Stephanus in sellam suam revertitur = Stephanus returns to his chair
Cōnsīde = Sit down
Ad sellam reversus Stephanus cōnsīdit = Having returned to the chair Stephanus sits down
Sequere mē = Follow me
Stephanus puellam sequitur = Stephanus follows the girl
Puellam secūtus Stephanus cōnsīdit = Having followed the girl Stephanus sits down
Oblīvīscere Rosae Alexander = Forget Rose Alexander
Aliam puellam amā Alexander = Love another girl Alexander
Alexander Rosae oblīvīscitur = Alexander forgets Rose
Oblītus Rosae Alexander aliam puellam amat = Having forgotten Rose Alexander loves another girl
