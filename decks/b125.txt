Dē Multiplicī Temporis Dictiōne (Complex Time Expressions) = Varium et mūtābile semper tempus — “Time is always changing” — Virgil

Every day, when Alexander sees beautiful girls, he follows them = Cotīdiē, cum Alexander pulchrās puellās videt, eās sequitur
Every day, when the teacher shouts, the students row = Cotīdiē, cum magister clāmat, discipulī rēmigant
Yesterday, when she saw Nicholas, Veronica fell in love with him = Herī, ut Nicolāum vidit, Verōnica eius amōre capta est
