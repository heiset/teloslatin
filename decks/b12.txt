Mē vocant (My name is) = "Let the wise man bear the name of a madman if he seek wisdom more than is sufficient." —Horace

What is your name? = Quōmodo tē vocant?
They call me ___. = Mē vocant ___.
By what name are you called? = Quō nōmine appellāris?
I am called Julius. = Iūlius appellor.
First name = Praenōmen
Family name = Nōmen
Nickname/Surname (remember, in Latin the surname is just a nickname) = Cognōmen
Titus Maccius Plautus = Titus Maccius Plautus (Plautus=flat-footed!)
What country do you have? = Quam patriam habēs?
I am Syracusan = Syrācūsānus sum
Of what country are you? = Cuiās es?
Of what country are y'all? = Cuiātēs estis?
Where are you born? = Unde es ortus / orta? (m/f)
Where are you all born? = Unde estis ortī / ortae? (m/f)
I was born in North America. = Ortus/a sum ex Americā Septentriōnālī (m/f)
