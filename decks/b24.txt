Unde venīs? (From where do you come?) = “From where the sun rises to where it sets, there is no one whose acts can compare with mine” - Cicero, Tusc. Quaes., v. 17.

Where do you come from, Stephen? = Unde venīs, Stephane?
I come from there, from the garden = Illinc veniō, ex hortō
Stephen comes from there = Stephanus venit illinc

Where are you going away from, Titus? = Unde abīs, Tite?
I go away from the garden = Ab hortō abeō
Titus goes away from the garden = Titus ab hortō abit

Stephen goes out of the house = Stephanus exit domō

Where are you arriving, Titus? = Quō pervenīs, Tite?
I arrive home = Domum perveniō
Titus arrives home = Titus pervenit domum

Brother and mother are at home = Frāter et māter domī sunt

Where does Stephen arrive? = Quō pervenit Stephanus?
Stephen arrives at the carriage = Stephanus pervenit ad raedam
