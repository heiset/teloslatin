Emere, Vendere, Quantī? (Buying, Selling, Price) = Pretium rerum non ex verbīs sed ex usu pendet — “The price of things depends not on words but on use” — Seneca
Māla, māla, māla vendō! = Apples, apples, I sell apples
Quantī cōnstat hoc mālum? = How much does this apple cost?
Trium dēnāriōrum cōnstat = It costs three denarii
Cārum est! Magnī cōnstat! = It is expensive! It costs a lot
Nōn est cārum sed vīle! = It is not expensive but cheap
Istud mālum emere nōlō = I do not want to buy that apple
Crustula, crustula, crustula vendō! = Cookies, cookies, I sell cookies
Quantī cōnstat hoc crustulum? = How much does this cookie cost?
Duōrum dēnāriōrum cōnstat = It costs two denarii
Pretium vīle est! Parvī cōnstat! = The price is cheap! It costs little
Vērē vīle est pretium = The price is truly cheap
Hoc crustulum emere volō = I want to buy this cookie
X nōn emit mālum = X does not buy the apple
Y nōn vēndit mālum = Y does not sell the apple
Y vēndit crustulum = Y sells the cookie
X emit crustulum = X buys the cookie
