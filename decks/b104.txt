Participia Plūrālia (Plural Participles) = Dum agimus discimus — “While we act we learn” — proverb
Puerī scribunt = The boys write
Calamī puerōrum scribentium magnī sunt = The pens of the writing boys are big
Puerī scribentēs obdormīvērunt = The boys fell asleep while writing
Magister puerōs dormientēs videt = The teacher sees the sleeping boys
Magister puerīs dormientibus īrāscitur = The teacher becomes angry at the sleeping boys
Puerī legentēs ambulant = The boys walk while reading
Liber puerōrum legentium studium movet = The book of the reading boys moves their interest
Mulier puerōs legentēs videt = The woman sees the reading boys
Mulier puerīs legentibus nōn īrāscitur = The woman is not angry at the reading boys
Puerōs videō domō ēgredientēs = I see the boys leaving the house
Puerī domō ēgrediuntur = The boys leave the house
Puerōs videō dormientēs = I see the sleeping boys
Puerī dormiunt = The boys sleep
