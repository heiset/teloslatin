Dē Āctiōnibus Imminentibus (Ongoing Actions) = Dum agis discis — “While you act you learn” — proverb
Puer scribit = The boy writes
Puer scribēns obdormīvit = The boy fell asleep while writing
Calamus puerī scribentis magnus est = The pen of the writing boy is big
Magister puerum dormientem videt = The teacher sees the sleeping boy
Magister puerō dormientī īrāscitur = The teacher becomes angry at the sleeping boy
Puer legēns ambulat = The boy walks while reading
Liber puerī legentis studium movet = The book of the reading boy moves his interest
Māter puerum legentem videt = The mother sees the reading boy
Māter puerō legentī nōn īrāscitur = The mother is not angry at the reading boy
Puerum videō domō ēgredientem = I see the boy leaving the house
Puer domō ēgreditur = The boy leaves the house
Puerum dormientem videō = I see the sleeping boy
Puer dormit = The boy sleeps
