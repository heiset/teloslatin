Mē vocant (My name is) = "Let the wise man bear the name of a madman if he seek wisdom more than is sufficient." —Horace

What is your name (lit How do they call you) = Quōmodo tē vocant
They call me ___ = Mē vocant ___
By what name are you called = Quō nōmine appellāris
I am called Julius = Iūlius appellor
First name = Praenōmen
Family name = Nōmen
Nickname / Surname = Cognōmen
Titus Maccius Plautus (the cognomen means flat-footed) = Titus Maccius Plautus (cognōmen significat plantipedem)
What country do you have = Quam patriam habēs
I am Syracusan = Syrācūsānus sum
Of what country are you = Cuiās es
Of what country are you all = Cuiātēs estis
Where are you born (m/f) = Unde es ortus / orta
Where are you all born (m/f) = Unde estis ortī / ortae
I was born from North America = Ortus / orta sum ex Americā Septentriōnālī
