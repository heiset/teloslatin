Dē Instrūmentō et Modō (Means and Manner) = Ars longa, vita brevis — “Art is long, life is short” — Hippocrates

I smell with my nose = Nāsō meō olfaciō
I eat with my teeth = Dentibus meīs comedō
I read with my eyes = Oculīs meīs legō
I run with my feet = Pedibus meīs currō
I write with my hand = Manū meā scrībō
I hear with my ear = Aurē meā audiō
She learns by reading = Legendō discit
They praise by clapping = Plaudendō laudant
